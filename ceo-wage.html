What this means for Canada
The most powerful predictor of social unrest ever documented.
By January 3rd, the average Canadian CEO has already earned what the median worker will earn for the entire year. This ratio — 250x — is one of the strongest predictors of social unrest ever documented in economic literature. When workers perceive the system as fundamentally unfair, trust in institutions collapses, populist movements surge, and social contracts break down. Combined with the housing crisis, this creates a compounding inequality trap where wealth compounds for those who own assets and stagnates for those who don't.

CEO compensation data from CCPA (Canadian Centre for Policy Alternatives) annual report "Canada's CEO Pay". Median worker wage from StatCan Labour Force Survey. Ratio calculation: average total compensation (salary + bonus + stock) of 100 highest-paid Canadian CEOs vs. median full-time worker annual wage. Published annually — most recent: January 2025.
Methodology
Score based on CEO:median worker pay ratio. Ratio 40x = 0.10. Ratio 100x = 0.35. Ratio 150x = 0.55. Ratio 200x = 0.70. Ratio 250x = 0.78 — Critical. Trajectory (rising) adds 0.05. Source: CCPA CEO Pay Report 2025.
