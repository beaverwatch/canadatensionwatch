What this means — and what it does NOT mean
The speed of change is what matters — not the change itself.
Immigration is not inherently destabilizing — Canada has always been built by successive waves of newcomers. What this indicator tracks is whether the speed of demographic change outpaces Canada's integration capacity.

When the proportion changes by 2–3% per year, institutions, schools, neighbourhoods and labour markets can adapt. When it changes by 5–8% per year — as it has since 2020 — the adaptation systems are overwhelmed. This is not a values judgment. It is a capacity question. Integration policy quality, housing availability and social services determine whether this shift becomes a crisis or a successful demographic renewal.

🔗 Why this indicator is rising so fast
01
Record immigration levels — Canada admitted 465,000 permanent residents in 2023 and 485,000 in 2024. These are the highest levels in Canadian history, far exceeding the 250,000–300,000 historically targeted. Source: IRCC 2024.
02
Native-born fertility collapse — at 1.25 children per woman, Canadian-born families are having far fewer children. This mechanically increases the proportion of births from immigration-origin families even without any change in immigration volumes.
03
Age structure difference — recent immigrants tend to be younger and at peak family-forming age. This demographic advantage amplifies their share of annual births beyond their share of total population.
04
Policy gap — immigration policy has accelerated without proportional investment in integration — housing, language training, employment recognition, social services. The mismatch is documented by the Auditor General of Canada 2024.

⚠️ Data Transparency & Important Note
Data from StatCan Census 2024 and Vital Statistics. "Immigration-origin families" = at least one parent born outside Canada. This indicator is tracked as a measure of demographic velocity — not as a judgment on immigration or immigrants. CASTOR.WATCH does not take a position on immigration levels or policy. We track the speed of change relative to integration capacity. Source: StatCan Table 17-10-0008-01 and Census 2021/2024 cross-tabulations.
Methodology
Score based on proportion of births from immigration-origin families AND annual rate of change. Under 25% = 0.15. 25–35% = 0.30. 35–40% = 0.45. 40–45% = 0.55. Annual growth rate above 2%/year adds 0.05. Current 42.3% + rapid growth = score 0.55 — Shifting / High.
