Why we track this — and why it is NOT about theology
Religion is tracked as a social cohesion proxy — not a theological judgment.
This indicator has nothing to do with whether religion is true, good or desirable. It is tracked because historically, religious institutions have provided social infrastructure — community networks, mutual aid, shared moral frameworks, meaning and purpose. When this infrastructure collapses rapidly, it creates a vacuum. That vacuum is always filled by something — sometimes secular equivalents, sometimes political ideologies, sometimes destructive movements. The speed of decline matters more than the level.

🟡 WATCH — Predicted cascade 10 to 20 years

years
Community infrastructure loss
Religious organizations provide 35% of Canada's social services — food banks, shelters, seniors care, addiction recovery. As congregations shrink, this infrastructure disappears without secular replacement. Source: Cardus Social Economics Project 2024.
10–15
years
Ideological vacuum fills with substitutes
When traditional meaning-making structures disappear, people seek replacements — political ideologies, wellness cults, online communities, conspiracy movements. This is documented in every post-religious society studied. Source: Pew Research Global Religion 2023.
10–20
years
Mental health system overwhelmed
Religious participation is one of the strongest protective factors against depression and suicide across all demographic groups. As it declines, demand on secular mental health systems increases beyond capacity. Source: American Journal of Psychiatry meta-analysis 2023.

Methodology
Score based on % saying religion is very important AND rate of decline. 45%+ = 0.10. 35% = 0.25. 25% = 0.48 (warning threshold). Below 20% = 0.65. Trajectory (declining) adds 0.05. Current 25% + rapid decline = score 0.48 — Watch.
