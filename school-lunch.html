🔗 How this indicator works
01
Family food budget is cut first — before rent, utilities or clothing. Children's lunches are often the first visible signal of household financial stress.
02
Breakfast Club registrations rise — parents can no longer send children to school with a full meal. This is the earliest institutional signal available.
03
School performance deteriorates — hungry children show measurable increases in attention difficulties, behavioral problems and absenteeism within 4-6 weeks.
04
Child welfare interventions increase — documented +7% in CPS/DPJ interventions for every +10% rise in Breakfast Club enrollment. Source: Food Banks Canada 2024.

⚠️ Data Transparency Note
The score of 0.73 is derived from Breakfast Club of Canada annual report data (2025) and Food Banks Canada HungerCount 2024. These are annual publications — not real-time data. Google Trends is used as a weekly proxy between publications. This indicator is updated annually when new reports are published.
Methodology
Composite score based on: Breakfast Club of Canada — 880,000 children served, +30% demand increase (50%) + Food Banks Canada HungerCount 2024 — 2.5M children in food insecure households (30%) + Google Trends proxy "school breakfast program canada" (20%). Correlation documented: Food Banks Canada 2024 — +7% CPS/DPJ interventions per +10% enrollment increase. This is the strongest predictive indicator on CASTOR.WATCH for child welfare outcomes.
