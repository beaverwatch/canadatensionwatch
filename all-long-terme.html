📊 Why These Indicators
Selected based on historical evidence: in societies that experienced major social upheaval — including the USSR collapse, post-war Europe, and Latin American crises of the 1980s — these demographic and economic signals appeared years before the crisis became visible. No ideology. No political agenda. Just patterns that have repeated across history. We track them because Canada is showing several of these signals simultaneously — for the first time.
👶 Demographics
🍼
Total Fertility Rate
"Canada is no longer reproducing itself."
StatCan · Demographic Statistics 2025
1.25
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 10–20 years
At 1.25 children per woman — well below the 2.1 replacement rate — Canada cannot sustain its population without immigration. Pension systems, healthcare and social programs all depend on a growing working-age population. This is the most structurally dangerous indicator on this dashboard. Every society that has sustained a fertility rate below 1.3 for more than 10 years has experienced serious economic and social disruption within 15–20 years.
👶
Infant Mortality Rate
"A plateau where decline should continue."
StatCan · Vital Statistics 2025
4.8
🟡 WATCH
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–15 years
4.8 per 1,000 births. The rate has plateaued since 2018 — it is no longer improving. In all societies studied, when infant mortality stops declining, it signals rising stress in vulnerable populations — poverty, inadequate healthcare access and social inequality. Not a crisis alone, but a warning signal when combined with other indicators.

Exogenous Birth Rate
"42.3% of births from immigration-origin families."
StatCan · Census 2024
42.3%
🟠 SHIFTING
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 10–20 years
42.3% of births in Canada are from families of immigrant origin. This is not inherently negative — but the speed of the shift matters. When demographic composition changes faster than social integration systems can absorb, historical evidence shows increased social tension, identity conflict and political polarization. Integration policy quality will determine the outcome.
🎓 Education & Gender
🎓
Gender Education Gap
"Women 71% vs Men 57% of university graduates."
StatCan · Education Indicators 2025
71/57
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–15 years
Women now represent 71% of university graduates vs 57% for men — a gap that is widening every year. Men without post-secondary education earn 40% less than women with degrees. Historical evidence from other countries shows this produces: rising male unemployment, delayed marriage, lower birth rates, and increased political radicalization among young men. This is not a women's issue — it is a structural social stability issue.

Male Suicide Rate Gap
"Men die by suicide 3x more than women — and the gap is widening."
StatCan · Causes of Death 2024
3x
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–10 years
Canadian men die by suicide at 3 times the rate of women — and the gap has been widening since 2019. This is one of the most reliable leading indicators of male social marginalization. Societies where this gap exceeds 3:1 consistently show rising male criminality, political extremism and social withdrawal within 5–10 years. This signal was present in the USSR before its collapse and in several Latin American economies before major social crises.
💰 Economy & Inequality
🏠
Housing Affordability Ratio
"Home price exceeds 9x annual median income in major cities."
CMHC · Housing Market Assessment 2025
>9x
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–15 years
A home in Toronto or Vancouver now costs more than 9 times the median annual income. The historical danger threshold is 5x — Canada crossed it years ago and keeps climbing. When housing becomes inaccessible to the middle class, wealth becomes locked in the hands of existing property owners — dramatically widening the gap between rich and poor. Young Canadians who cannot buy property will never build the same wealth as their parents. This creates a permanent two-tier society: owners and renters. Historically, when this ratio exceeds 8x for more than 5 years, societies experience: mass emigration of young talent, collapsing birth rates, rising political populism, and eventual housing market crashes that devastate the middle class.
👔
CEO vs Median Wage Ratio
"Canadian CEOs earn 250x the average worker."
CCPA / StatCan · Executive Compensation 2025
250x
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–15 years
The average Canadian CEO earns 250 times the median worker's salary — up from 40x in 1995. This ratio is one of the strongest predictors of social unrest ever documented. When workers perceive the economic system as fundamentally unfair, trust in institutions collapses, populist movements surge, and social contracts break down. Combined with the Housing Affordability crisis, this produces a compounding inequality trap: those who own assets get richer from inflation; those who don't fall further behind every year.
💳
BNPL Market Growth
"Buy Now Pay Later — growing 16.5% annually."
ResearchAndMarkets · Canadian BNPL Report 2026
+16.5%
🟠 HIGH
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 3–10 years
The Canadian BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) market is growing at 16.5% per year and is now worth $9.5 billion. BNPL is the new payday loan for the middle class — it normalizes debt for everyday purchases. When a society routinely finances groceries, clothing and small electronics on credit, it signals that wages are no longer sufficient for normal life. Historically, rapid BNPL growth precedes consumer debt crises within 5–8 years.
🏛️ Society & Institutions
🍁
National Identity Disaffiliation
"Only 40% of Canadians aged 18–34 say they are proud to be Canadian."
Leger / Environics Institute 2024
40%
🔴 CRITICAL
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–15 years
Only 40% of Canadians aged 18–34 say they are proud to be Canadian — compared to 78% of those aged 65+. National identity is the invisible glue that holds collective sacrifice together — taxes, military service, social programs all require a shared sense of "we." When young generations abandon this identity, the political will to maintain collective institutions collapses. This is not a patriotism question. It is a social cohesion question.

🟡 WATCH

25% of Canadians say religion is very important — exactly at the historical warning threshold of 25–30%. The speed of decline matters more than the absolute level.
🗳️
Political Donation Surge
"Political donations up +50% in 5 years."
Elections Canada · Political Finance Reports 2025
+50%
🟠 HIGH
⏱️ Predicted Impact — 5–10 years
Total political donations in Canada have increased by more than 50% in 5 years. When political donations surge, it typically signals one of two things: rising political engagement OR rising capture of political institutions by economic interests. Combined with the CEO wage ratio and housing crisis, this pattern suggests that political power is increasingly concentrated among those with economic resources — a historically reliable precursor to democratic erosion and populist backlash.
⚠️ Transparency & Limitations
Long-term indicators are updated annually when official data is published. Scores represent our assessment of structural risk based on historical patterns — they are not predictions of specific events. No indicator alone is determinative. The danger signal comes from multiple indicators moving simultaneously in the same direction — which is what we are observing in Canada in 2026.
