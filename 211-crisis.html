🔗 How this indicator works
01
Someone calls 211 — they have exhausted all other options. 211 is the social safety net of last resort in Canada. Every call represents a real person in genuine crisis.
02
Housing is the #1 unmet need — 35% of callers need housing help and cannot get it. This is not a new problem — but it is not improving despite government programs.
03
Unmet needs cascade — when housing needs go unmet, food insecurity, mental health crises and family breakdown follow within weeks. 211 data captures the leading edge of this cascade.
04
Visible homelessness follows — persistent unmet housing needs through 211 precede increases in visible street homelessness by 4 to 12 weeks. Source: Canadian Observatory on Homelessness.

⚠️ Data Transparency Note
211 data is published quarterly by 211 Canada and provincial networks. The score of 0.72 is derived from the February 2026 national dashboard showing 35% unmet housing needs (rank #1) and Ontario contact volume of 301,583. No single national volume figure exists — each province manages its own 211 network. Ontario is used as the national proxy given its size and data quality.
Methodology
Composite score: 211 Canada unmet housing need ratio (35% = 0.72 on 0-1 scale, weight 60%) + 211 Ontario contact volume trend (stable/rising = elevated signal, weight 40%). Score of 0.72 reflects persistent high unmet need with no improvement vs prior year. Source: 211 Canada National Dashboard, February 2026 + 211 Ontario Annual Data 2024-2025. Updated quarterly when 211 Canada publishes new data.
