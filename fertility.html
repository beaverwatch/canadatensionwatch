What this means for Canada
Canada cannot replace its own population.
At 1.25 children per woman — far below the 2.1 replacement rate — Canada's population would shrink by approximately 40% within three generations without immigration. This is not a future projection. It is happening now. Every social program Canada has built — pensions, healthcare, education, social housing — was designed around a growing population. A shrinking native-born population creates a structural funding crisis that no political party is currently equipped to solve.

Canada will require 400,000–500,000 immigrants per year just to maintain economic stability. The speed of demographic change will outpace integration capacity — historically a trigger for social conflict.

Economic contraction
Japan sustained a fertility rate of 1.2–1.3 for 30 years. The result: two lost decades of economic growth, a debt-to-GDP ratio of 260%, and a society unable to fund its own future. Canada is 15 years behind Japan on this curve.
🔗 Why fertility is falling
01
Housing unaffordability — young Canadians cannot afford the space needed for a family. A 2-bedroom apartment in Toronto averages $3,200/month. Many couples delay or abandon having children due to housing costs.
02
Student debt burden — average Canadian university graduate carries $28,000 in student debt. Debt delays family formation by an average of 4.2 years. Source: StatCan Survey of Financial Security.
03
Cultural shift — a growing proportion of young Canadians are choosing to remain childfree. This is documented across all G7 countries and appears to be a permanent, not temporary, demographic shift.
04
Economic anxiety — Canadians cite economic insecurity as the #1 reason for having fewer children than desired. Source: Angus Reid Institute 2024.

Methodology
The Total Fertility Rate (TFR) measures the average number of children a woman would have over her lifetime given current age-specific fertility rates. A TFR of 2.1 is the replacement rate — the level needed to maintain population size without immigration. Canada's TFR of 1.25 is among the lowest ever recorded for a major economy. Score conversion: TFR 2.1+ = 0.0 (no risk). TFR 1.8 = 0.30. TFR 1.5 = 0.60. TFR 1.25 = 0.90 (critical). Linear interpolation between thresholds.
